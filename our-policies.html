Our Policies

At Old Brooklyn Computer Company, we're committed to delivering quality computer repair services to our valued customers. To maintain a seamless and transparent experience, we've outlined the following policies:

1. Diagnostic Fee

At Old Brooklyn Computer Company, we typically provide free estimates. However, for more complex issues that require in-depth diagnostics, such as when disassembly is necessary to pinpoint the problem, a modest diagnostic fee may be charged. If you choose to proceed with the recommended repairs, this fee will be applied toward the total repair cost.

2. Repair Estimates

Before we begin any repair work, you'll receive a detailed estimate outlining the necessary tasks, parts, and associated costs. We prioritize clear and transparent pricing, and we will always seek your approval before moving forward with any repairs.

3. Repair Timeframe

We know how crucial your computer is to you, so our technicians strive to complete repairs as swiftly as possible. However, the time required to finish repairs can vary depending on the complexity of the issue and parts availability.

4. Customer Authorization

Your explicit authorization is required before we proceed with any repairs or order any parts beyond what is specified in the initial estimate.

5. Data Protection

We treat your data and personal information with the highest level of confidentiality. While we take all necessary precautions to protect your privacy, we strongly encourage you to back up your important data before bringing in your device. Please read our Privacy Policy for more details.

6. Payment Terms

Payment is due once the repair is completed. We accept various payment methods, including cash, credit/debit cards, and others as agreed upon.

7. Important Notice

The total amount due for parts and labor must be paid within 15 days of the repair completion date. After this period, a storage/late fee of $15 per week will be applied. If a system is not picked up within 60 days after the work is completed, or if we lose contact with the customer (whichever comes first), it will be deemed abandoned and will become property of Old Brooklyn Computer Company.
